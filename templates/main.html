About our Project

Malaria is a rapidly spreading and life-threatening disease infected through the mosquito into the blood. In short, nearly half the worldâ€™s population is at risk from malaria and there are over 200 million malaria cases and approximately 400,000 deaths due to malaria every year. Malaria Virus damages the oxygen carrying RBC cells . The standard way of treatment to malaria is by visually examining blood via microscope for parasite-infected red blood cells. This method is time consuming and inefficient. This diagnosis depend on the experience of the examiner. Automatic image recognition is previously implemented to malaria blood smears for treatment before. Coming to practical this model gave less accuracy. This give us to study more on image recognition technology for malaria detection. Our aim of the project is build a model that predict the malaria with best accuracy . we trained the dataset with CNN algorithm. Model is trained by the collection of microscopic images of blood smears collected from the various people. t is classified as uninfected and infected with early and effective testing using CNN algorithm and also perform classification on the infected cells using Deep Learning .

CNN Algorithm

The image is read by the computer using RGB channels, each of which has its own set of pixel values. If an image has p rows, q columns, and n channels, it is called a A*B*C image. CNN stands for convolutional neural network. This algorithm is based on deep learning. The visual cortex in the brain inspires the interactions of neurons in a CNN, which is a form of feed forward artificial neural network. The visual cortex is a tiny portion of the brain that is responsible for vision.
